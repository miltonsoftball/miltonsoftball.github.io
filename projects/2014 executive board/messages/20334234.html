Kevin Olivieri on January 18, 2014:

As a follow-up to our conversation a few weeks ago, I did some additional digging on the USPS EveryDoorDirectMail Service.  You can select a zip code as a starter.  From there, you are able to select neighborhoods you would like to target.  Neighborhoods are determined by the number of postal carriers required to delivery mail in a zip code.  Milton has over 30 neighborhoods.  When I selected all the Milton Neighborhoods, I could reach 9.195 addresses.

I could also overlay some demographics such as household income ranges, male/female, age, and if they had children.  I selected the children option.  Now although I CANNOT target JUST addresses with children, I am able to see what neighborhoods have a greater propensity for this criteria (see graphic).

If we were to target all 9,195 Milton/02186 addresses, it would cost $3,402.15 (37¢ per address).  A 2-3% response rate is the industry standard on direct mail.  This equates to 183 families registering to play softball.  It's not a realistic expectation because I think for a lot of our addresses/households the mailer would be irrelevant.

If we could identify neighborhoods that had low softball registrations, maybe we could target those neighborhoods.  This got me thinking that it would be interesting to see on a map the neighborhoods of the 381 girls who played softball in 2013 (see attached).  In studying this map, it seems like there are two neighborhoods that are low with softball players:
  1. Area between Brush Hill and Truman Parkway
  2. Indian Springs
If you want to zoom in and interact with the map, click here:
http://batchgeo.com/map/ce41eb13c65d620f731551854dcb875c

This would be about 500 homes at a cost of $185.  We would need 2 registrations to break even.  I am not sure we NEED to do it, but it's an option to consider.  

The turnaround time with the post office is about 10 business days.

What do you think?

Kevin Olivieri on January 20, 2014:

I worked over the weekend and set up an experiment with Google Keywords.  Under a free promotion, we have purchased keywords so when someone in the Boston DMA (essentially metro Boston) searches for keywords we define on google.com, an ad for Milton Girls Softball will appear.  We only pay when someone clicks on it at a cost is 2¢ per click.  The cap is $1.25 per day and we have a free credit on the account to work from that will allow us to determine if this is a viable channel for us.  

In looking at our Google Analytics (web traffic), I think it may be something we continue to do.  I will post more details under a post we can share with the entire board.
