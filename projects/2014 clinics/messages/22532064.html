Pitching Open House Clinic

Posted by Kevin Olivieri on March 3, 2014

An email was sent out to all registered members about a free open house clinic happening Friday, March 7th at the Milton High School friend house. The clinic will be run by Janelle Carlson with support from the Milton High School Varsity Softball team.

I am unable to attend, but it would be great to have some board members present so please make an effort to attend if you can.  

Thanks to Gary and Bill for pulling this together.

Details are below.

Milton Girls Softball is excited to offer a free, open house pitching clinic on Friday, March 7th from 6pm-8pm at the Milton High School Field House.  

This clinic is sponsored by Milton Girls Softball and will be run by Pitching Coach Janelle Carlson with the support of the Milton High School Girls Varsity Softball team.

If your daughter would like to learn what pitching is all about or has pitched in the past, this clinic is geared towards beginners to advanced athletes in Grades 3 through 8.  

The professional pitching Instructors will cover the basic fundamental mechanics of pitching:
what you need to learn to become a pitcher
lessons to learn and practice at home
how to increase speed

Date:  March 7th
Time: 6-8pm
Place: Milton High School Field House
Cost: Free
What to Bring: Bring your glove and a catcher.

Gary Kelly on March 8, 2014:

Thanks to all the board members and coaches in helping make the Clinic a success last night.

The turnout was good, we had 22 girls register at the door, expectations were for at least 40,  but for the size of the group, it worked out great.  I expected more with Eddie Baker sneaking it in his article at the last minute for me. Considering I locked down the facility late, and we went out with a notice late, overall it was fine, especially where our total cost was $20 !!  Last nights venue would have either costed the attendees, or the program a total expense of over $500.

We introduced everyone, then had the Coach Losi and the HS Varsity girls teach them how to warm up and throw.  Then Janelle took over, and did a great job.  Parents on the way out went out of their way to thank us and say how much they liked it.

A number of things we learned, if we did have a group of 40- 60 , we would need enough instructors to break off into groups of 12-16 kids, so 4 to 5 instructors, which is doable as the basic instruction is the same, (grip, snaps, butterly, clock, around the world, this all takes about an hour).  

The group was mostly 3rd and 4th graders, this was the most rewarding part of the clinic, as we want to target the younger girls.

Bill and I will do a full recap for we can build on this.  The takeaway for me is to find space for kids to either find a reason to learn, or a reason to practice, if we have the venues, more kids will either try to learn, or keep the ones trying, practicing.
