>>> Hi guys, we just met and discussed some rules proposals that we recommend before next weeks meetings begin:
>>>
>>> Sophomore pitching: we are recommending that during the regular season, there is a 5 walk maximum per inning per pitcher. After the fifth walk, the opposing coach would come in to finish the inning pitching to his or her own team. The girl who has been taken out is still allowed to re-enter the game in any subsequent inning. As we discussed, coaches at no point are allowed to walk their own players, and will also continue to pitch the first 2 innings of every game. However, we are recommending that for the playoffs, coaches will not relieve a girl after 5 walks in an inning, and also would not pitch the first two innings either. So, no coaches pitching in the playoffs. The rational behind this is that by June, pitching skills should improve and rotations tightened to the point where the girls should control their own destiny on the mound. Also, many of the girls who pitch will be playing more competitive summer ball a week later, and preparing them for better competition would prove beneficial to both them and the program.
>>>
>>> We also propose as a recommendation, not a rule, that for all divisions, the league provide lightweight pitching cages/masks that would be available for all pitchers to use. We suggest buying one per team, and making it part of the equipment bag. We should announce it to all parents that this is available. The thinking here is that for relatively short money, maybe $300-$500, we would provide a safety mechanism, which if it saves one nose or set of teeth, would be well worth it. Optional, but available.
>>>
>>> We propose clarifying and enforcing the rule that for all divisions, outfielders need to be on the outfield grass, not on the dirt and outfield border. This came up in playoff games in both sophomore and junior divisions...funny only in playoff games.
>>>
>>> Finally, we want to clarify and recommend the suggestion at the board meeting that for the sophomore division, only 6 girls are required to start the game, with 8 finishing. What was not discussed amongst the rules committee, but just popped into my head while typing, is how to we treat the game where 6 start, and less than 8 finish, despite expectations of late arrivals, My suggestion is that the coaches decide at home plate amongst themselves before the game as to whether or not the game will be official should the 7th and/or 8th girl not show up. My prediction is that in every instance, the coaches agree to play the game and let the results stand, which at this level, serves everybody's best interest.
