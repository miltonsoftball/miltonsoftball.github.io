Gary Kelly on May 5, 2014:

I had a discussion with Bill last night on some thoughts and ideas on Summer ball.  Dont want to write a book here, so will let Bill take away whatever he thought from a big picture standpoint, along with the shorter term tactical thoughts.  Listed below are just a few of what I think might be more critical. 

* Do you repackage summer, and communicate a change- i.e. - The first team will be the A team, a summer all star team, which will have different expectations of our regular travel program.  A combination of Travel and a minimum of 2 or 3 tourneys.  A vast difference in Commitment (something that is lacking in the summer program)
*A boots on the ground campaign for coaches- Bill started this with a good email going out to spring coaches.  This does not change much from years past, but a focus needs to be on the upcoming coaches of who is eager to have their child continue to play at a higher level, and mingle in the sandbox.
*You hope summer coaches are some of the better coaches in the program- it attracts and builds for future teams.
*Outsourcing Tryouts has alot of pros going for it.  Probably a good idea.  If its done, I believe the timing should be sensitive towards the view if its creating more of an elitist persona, and kids are less apt to come try out.  (maybe thats okay to, depending on the numbers expected, and objective)
*Evaluations in house have come along way (thanks to a great deal of Kevins work with organizing the formats and a joint goal of trying to make it a quantitative and qualitative process.  In house I think continues to help keep the tryout community closer knit and possibly allow to build and attract.
*We are paying the price for a strategic decision (years back) to have the Travel league be more accommodating to summer schedules and try to attract quantity, and not so much quality (except for team one picked).  We have come along way, 12 years ago, we had usually 2 or 3 travel teams.  Over the last 7-8 years, we have been as high as 7, and been averaging 6.  I guess we can take the positive road,  and realize lots more kids playing summer ball. (this is where the that whole big picture, mission and objective of summer probably needs a little more help)

So much for not writing a book...
