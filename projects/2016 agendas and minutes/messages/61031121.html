Gile Field Lighting Update...

Posted by Bill Giovannucci on August 16, 2016

Hey All,

I just wanted to pass along that it looks like there may be some recent progress on the Gile lights situation.  The baseball guys put together a meeting yesterday up at Gile with the Parks Dept., an Eversource Rep and engineer, as well as a local electrician.  

The fall out was this new Eversource contact seems amenable to the electrician's proposal they eliminate the current master meter.  Instead, each set of field lights would be metered as a separate account.  Most of the work would be done in the brick power house.   This plan would avoid costly trenching back to the street for new service lines for each field.   This was likely an engineering option the utility co ignored years ago for reasons unknown, (...maybe technology or policy reasons even).   

Regardless, this could be good news on the way for all who use the field lights.    Eversource still has to process the proposal.  They may take up to 60 days to review and respond to the electricians work order.   Besides individual control over lighting and scheduling, the big gain for all would be that no meter would be likely to top the 10,000 kw use threshold in a billing cycle.  That inflexible rate structure is what causes the monthly demand charges to skyrocket (...I am told the demand charge at least triples for an entire billing period any time the threshold is met for even one day.). 

Also, the Parks Dept was very interested in converting to LED.   This projects to save approx.  60 - 80% off the KW usage by just replacing the existing metal-halide bulbs.   The bulbs could be very pricey up front.   I guess we'll have wait to see how the Parks Dept. proposes to pay for that, or if any municipal rebate deals exist for the Town possibly.   This change to LED would however come with a separate, great long term cost benefit.   Using the lights would be much, much more affordable per hour for many years ahead. 

Should be more news on this in about 30 - 60 days.  I do know the electrician involved well enough to stay in contact with him directly.    As an aside, I will follow up with him for pricing the power to the approved scoreboard location, as well as to the backstop.   The thinking being, if this guy does end up doing the meter work maybe we dovetail in our other work while his attention is on Gile.  At the very least,  I'll aim to stay informed for MGS as long as I have access to the guys driving this.

Back to school in about two weeks! Enjoy the rest of the summer everyone!

-Bill Gio.
