Summer Tryouts - By The Numbers

Posted by Kevin Olivieri on May 1, 2016

Over 90 girls attended tryouts on Saturday. Record number for our program. Congrats!!

We have some key decisions to make on teams by each U-Level. Let's start by looking at the numbers:
U18 - TOTAL: 11+: 11 committed to playing. Few others in the mix.
U14 - TOTAL 28: 26 at tryouts, 2 were away. Of the 28, 3 are U12s asking to play up on U14 
U12 - TOTAL 38: 32 at tryouts, 5 were away, 1 was a no show and did not let league know she could not make it.*  Of the 38, 3 are U10s asking to play up on U12
U10 - TOTAL 36: 25 at tryouts, 5 were away/injured, 6 were a no show and did not let league know she could not make it.* 

Teams:
U18
  • 1 team

U14
  • 2 teams (how many per team? 12 or 13?)
  • Are we cutting girls?
  • Do the U12 girls get pushed to their age appropriate division?

U12:
  • 3 teams? 
  • Do we have 1 Elite and 2 balanced teams? OR 3 in same division (if allowed)? How many per team? 11, 12 or 13? 
  • Are we cutting girls?  
  • Do the U12 girls get pushed to their age appropriate division?

U10
  • 2 teams? 3 teams? 
  • Do we have 1 Elite and 2 balanced teams? OR 3 in same division (if allowed)? Or 2 teams and the 3rd is a more formalized Summer Slam that Sally did last summer with practices and scrimmages with other U10s and possible scrimmages with the towns?
  • Hate to cut girls (especially when the number gets to 8 or more. We may lose them for good). 
  • How many per team? 11, 12 or 13? Are we cutting girls?  

A LOT TO THINK ABOUT. LET THE CONVERSATION BEGIN. 

PLEASE WEIGH IN AS WE ARE TARGETING FRIDAY TO HAVE THIS DEFINED. 


*NOTE:
I have a POV that if a player does not show up for tryouts and does not inform the program before tryouts that she is unable to attend, MGS do not have an obligation to hold a spot for her. I consider her a NSNE (No Show, No Excuse). We can offer her a spot if we need the numbers, but in my opinion we are not obligated. I would love to hear your thoughts about this.

Bill Giovannucci on May 2, 2016:

I'm new to all these roster dynamics, so I expect others have more experience; 

-I do agree with a soft NSNE  policy. 

-It seems there are smaller % of stand-out skill players overall.  Most are about in the middle I would say. 

-If three teams would be allowed, I think it would be best for us keep more kids playing in some way.    In that case  ...I would lean toward one elite team based on the try out results.    Split the talent up between the two others even if that creates two so-so rosters.   The summer league last season I saw was not at all over the heads of our mediocre kids.  They tried out for a competitive spot.  Challenging them should be fine if we are allowed.   I suppose if we had to have one competitive scrimmage league team then I'd still do that, but try to make it somehow an equal (even if different) experience to the other "B" team. 

-Maybe the playing up decision should stay just a case by case basis, depending more on the logistics of roster needs in the overall picture, and of course safety.    I think kids should be pushed to work over their heads, as long as they are at the same time coached to handle the experience (and there always exists a possibility for even small successes).   Working "just above" perceived or actual is a fundamental standard.   However, often the most skilled, by the very nature of their exceptional abilities, undervalue the important lessons of leadership and "Team".  Sometimes that is just because its harder to coach those things.   How many girls are there really who wouldn't be learning something in either situation? Parents want what is best for their kids.  If there is merit in that explanation to all and ultimately in their daughters experience, then in the end you won't have gone wrong.

MAB Reference (discussion for next season.....)
Kevin had noted he had discussions about other examples of summer programs, notably with MAB.  First hand experience, the families had options of 1 of 3 approach's for tryout's;

1) Register for A (Elite?) team, with commitment of 3-4 tournaments and practices/games 4 days a week or
2) Register for A or B (accept whatever placement the player achieves)
2) Register for B only (larger rosters, less commitment and 1 tournament)

MGS Approach - on summer tryouts, indicate if trying out for South Shore travel league only, or the travel league + Elite/Tournament Team (note the expectations, # of practices, tournaments etc.)

PROPOSAL
1) Field as many summer travel teams as possible (U10, U12) for regular week-day season.  
2) Balance teams, believe our skill level will be fine in upper third of the South Shore league versus the Hockomock league
2) Single U10/12 Elite or "Tournament Team" based on tryouts, play in 3-4 tournaments from Memorial Day through July, satisfies level of play/interest

Benefits
- Expose more girls to summer play, enhance their game awareness and experience.
- Spring rec league play increases in its level of competition
- Summer team experiences the camaraderie and enjoyment of the game
