Gary Kelly on September 16, 2015:

I spoke with Terry Driscoll yesterday (prior Milton Park Commissioner and Owner of Read Custom soils- provides material to fenway park and area Colleges).  He was very glad to hear that the company STS is doing the work, says they are the best in the NE, and last year went down to PA to help with the Williamsburg little league field before the World series.

Terry mentioned the soil being applied is one of the best that can be used, and its all about the mixture, and feels the right mix is being used based on how it will be kept up.  He did mention, we should inquire about the irrigation as this is the time to correct it, if its not being corrected to hit the material. (ie. our dusty issues can be minimized with the correct irrigation design).  My two cents on that, would be to hold Perdios accountable to fix/change for that to happen, and that is a cost that should be absorbed by his dept (as it goes to help his maintenance and helps his dept as well as playing conditions for us).

Gary Kelly on September 16, 2015:

My vote as mentioned at the meeting is Yes.  We have been talking about capital planning for years, and as we have been making investments each year in certain areas, these type of expenses are the type that can arise quickly and usually need quick action, its that nature of the non profit (volunteer) beast.  

If I could suggest to maybe create a reserve/restricted account, which is set up to allocate funds each year from the general budget (on a % basis), for capital or long range projects and it continues to reduce the amount of retained earnings on hand at the end of each year (which should be less than 15-20%).  Then these funds are already ratified for either issues that arise quickly to take advantage of, or helps us plan for things like a scoreboard.

Lastly, I do feel that with the fields being town fields and open to the public, (along with permit fees being charged), that the Park Department should be footing the complete cost, but in today's world, since they charge us a fee to use the fields, they (town $$) could be paying for half, and the other half should be coming out of the mysac fees.  Since that is not happening, and we charge town kids to play softball, and we are asked to help with the cost, the total cost that remains should be absorbed by our program.
