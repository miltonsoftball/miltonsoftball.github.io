Kevin Olivieri on March 13, 2013:

I had a conversation with Laurie and expressed the feedback we had been getting regarding her role within the program.  I told her that while it's great that she attends games, she needs to avoid inserting herself into games regarding rules, rosters, etc.  She understood and expressed to me that she acknowledges that she needs to not get involved.  I told her that as a coach, board member and (at the time) the Junior Division Coordinator, she cannot leverage these roles during her games or during games she attends.  With that, I offered a Senior Division coaching position to her for the 2012 season.  

On anther note, I emailed Arleen regarding her interest in coaching (without offering the exact role).  She replied back to me Wednesday afternoon (from my Sunday email) and told me that she said "I travel quite a bit and am concerned that I will not have the time to devote to it.  I have not decided just yet but will let you know as soon as I decide.  If you have someone else that you think can devote the time you may want to go with her/him."  Based on this response I feel she will not be able to dedicate the time to being a Head Coach and that she is better suited in the role as an assistant.

We also received feedback on another option - Tony D'amato - and the feeling was that he would be a good assistant but that he has never been a head coach and throwing him into his first head coaching position in the Senior Division may result in a  not-so-positive experience for all.

The last coach on the list is Mark Ware.  I spoke to Mark today and discussed with him some of the good and some of the not so good feedback we have received over the past few seasons.  I looked back at survey feedback from last year as well as the year he coached the Junior Pirates and Summer U10? team (with Conroy).  There were several very positive comments about him and how he worked with the girls.  I also shared with him that some areas that require some focus occurred in the summer playoff game two years ago as well as this past season.  He did recognize that his behavior was not at it's best and he also pointed out that he gets competitive in the summer.  I agreed with him on that statement.  He is very excited, enthusiastic and very much looking forward to softball.  He also pointed out that this is the last season he will be coaching his daughter who moves on to high school next year.  

Taking into consideration the positives and negatives as well as feedback from Karen and Gary, I made the decision that Mark Ware would be offered a coaching position in the Senior Division with the stipulation that he needs to manage his emotions and keep his behavior on the field in check.  He accepted.

Mark and Laurie are enthusiastic and BIG supporters of the program. I wish we had other coaches and parents show this much excitement.  I am confident that they will demonstrate to us that we made the right decision.

I ask that outside of the three of us that we show an united front regarding the coaching selections.  

Feedback is welcome.
