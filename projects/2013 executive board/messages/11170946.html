Kevin Olivieri on May 24, 2013:

Thanks for including the scorecard evaluations.  I tabulated the data and there is a statistical issue with how the averages were calculated in your original rankings.  

You took the total score from each evaluator and then averaged the scores by dividing by the number of evaluators.  The issue is from looking at the scorecards we have some discrepancies.

Here is one example:
Evaluator A rated the following: Baserunning + Outfield + Throwing
Evaluator B rated the following: Baserunning + Infield + Throwing
Evaluator C rated the following: Baserunning + Hitting

Taking the data from one player, here are the numbers:
Evaluator A rated the following: Baserunning (2) + Outfield (3) + Throwing (3) == 10
Evaluator B rated the following: Baserunning (2) + Infield (2.5) + Throwing (2.5) == 7
Evaluator C rated the following: Baserunning (2) + Hitting (3) == 5

In your calculation, you took the 10, 7 and 5 and averaged it to determine a score.  This issue is that Evaluator C only evaluated 2 categories where the other two evaluated 3 categories.  This puts the player at a disadvantage because she is in fact missing a score.  It also reduces the value of Evaluator C since only 2 scores of his are being counted where the other evaluators have 3.  

We rectified this in the scorecard we have used the past three years by keeping each score as equal weight to give us a balanced rating.  It would account for evaluators missing ratings of some players without penalizing the girls.
