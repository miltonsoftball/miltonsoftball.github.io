I reviewed what Rich came up with as well as the Booster's and the Hock applications and guidelines. Gary I also looked at the list of things you sent me via email. I have a few questions/comments that I need to address before proposing the MGSA guidelines and application. I have a draft but want to see how you both feel about certain issues. Please let me know your thoughts on the following. I would prefer not to get a committee to come up with guidelines and application only because today is 3/28 and if we need a deadline or early May we need to get this out asap.

  • Do we have a statement that says we are a non-profit? If so I think we should include that and I would love to see the exact language.
  • I think it's a good idea to include our mission statement as well on the application. My reason for doing this is that we are asking the girls to write an essay and I want to see if they write about some of the goals of the mission statement in their essay.
  • One of the qualifications that Rich listed is "The Milton Girls Softball Association will be sponsoring two (2) annual $250 scholarships for graduating high school students who have participated in the MGSA for a minimum of four years and are planning to continue their education at a vocational school or college or university (two-year or four-year) or have enlisted in a branch of the US Armed Forces." I don't agree with the Armed Forces part of this...what are we giving them money for? school after the miliarty? or beer/party money? I know that they are doing a great thing for our country and I also know that giving someone money doesn't mean that they are going to put it toward their tuition or books but I think there's a better chance of that if the person is not only applied to but accepted and going to a college/university.
  • College you plan to attend, has the person applied and has the person been accepted--I think we should either ask for photocopy of the letter of acceptance or correspondence that they have committed to going to a school. How do we prove this? Hock asks the question but Booster's doesn't. I am not comfortable asking the question and then not getting proof.
  • What do you think of offering  it to a student that is accepted to a college, university, technical or vocational school--2 or 4 year? I think all of them are acceptable.
  • I don't think we should include personal references unless we intend to call them and I am not a firm believer in getting references. Why would someone put down a bad reference? The answer is they don't. Even terrible employees know people that will tell a white lie to as a favor.
  • Booster's asks for a parent's signature, should we require one or both?
  • Booster's asks for a letter of recommendation, should we require this? Boosters is very specific asking for that person to "please describe the nature of your association with this student, and please comment on how the student has demonstrated the qualities of the award, such as their success, determination, teamwork, leadership, sportsmanship, respect and commitment."
Gary you had mentioned Margaret Gibbons from MHS that manages the Booster's scholarships, do you think it's a good idea for me to call and/or meet with her to ask these types of questions. Please let me know and I can follow up with her on Monday.

Please let me know your thoughts and then I will finalize the applications and guidelines and send them to you tomorrow.

(15% of $6,700),, which would mean, we would need a General fund balance of about $33,500. (20% of 33500= $6700)
